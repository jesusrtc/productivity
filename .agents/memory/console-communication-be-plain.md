The user wants console communication to be **plain and simple**. They said my
recaps/summaries are too complicated to follow — too much jargon, too many
nested bullets and headers — even when they asked for the recap.

**Why:** they said "be clearer when you communicate in console" and that they
don't like the dense, hard-to-parse style.

**How to apply:** explain things in everyday language, at the what-it-does
level. Drop implementation jargon (API routes, function/field names, "status
lifecycle", child_process, etc.) unless they ask. Prefer a few short sentences
over long structured docs. Lead with the plain point. When confirming
understanding, ask only a few simple questions.
